Independent Auditor's Report on Financial Statements

Report to the Board of Directors and Shareholders
We have audited the accompanying consolidated financial statements of XYZ Corporation and its subsidiaries, which comprise the consolidated balance sheets as of December 31, 2024 and 2023, and the related consolidated statements of operations, comprehensive income, stockholders' equity, and cash flows for the years then ended, and the related notes to the consolidated financial statements.

Management's Responsibility for Financial Statements
Management is responsible for the preparation and fair presentation of these consolidated financial statements in accordance with accounting principles generally accepted in the United States of America. This includes the design, implementation, and maintenance of internal control relevant to the preparation and fair presentation of financial statements that are free from material misstatement, whether due to fraud or error.

Auditor's Responsibility
Our responsibility is to express an opinion on these consolidated financial statements based on our audits. We conducted our audits in accordance with auditing standards generally accepted in the United States of America. Those standards require that we plan and perform the audit to obtain reasonable assurance about whether the consolidated financial statements are free from material misstatement.

Internal Control Assessment
We assessed the effectiveness of the company's internal control over financial reporting as of December 31, 2024. Our assessment included testing of key controls over significant accounts and processes, evaluation of control design and operating effectiveness, and identification of material weaknesses or significant deficiencies.

Key Audit Areas
Our audit focused on several key areas including revenue recognition, inventory valuation, goodwill impairment testing, income tax provisions, and debt covenant compliance. We performed substantive testing procedures and analytical reviews to obtain sufficient appropriate audit evidence regarding these critical areas.

Revenue Recognition Analysis
We evaluated the company's revenue recognition policies and procedures to ensure compliance with ASC 606. Our testing included analysis of customer contracts, performance obligations, transaction price allocation, and timing of revenue recognition. We noted no material exceptions in our revenue recognition testing.

Inventory Management Review
We conducted physical inventory observations at major locations and tested inventory pricing, obsolescence reserves, and lower of cost or market adjustments. Our procedures included analytical reviews of inventory turnover ratios and comparison to industry benchmarks.

Goodwill and Intangible Assets
We evaluated management's annual goodwill impairment testing methodology and assumptions. Our procedures included assessment of reporting units, fair value calculations, and sensitivity analysis of key assumptions. We engaged valuation specialists to assist in our evaluation of complex fair value measurements.

Tax Compliance and Provisions
We reviewed the company's tax positions, uncertain tax positions, and deferred tax asset valuation allowances. Our procedures included analysis of tax returns, correspondence with tax authorities, and evaluation of tax planning strategies. We confirmed that tax provisions are appropriately stated in accordance with ASC 740.

Related Party Transactions
We identified and evaluated all related party transactions for proper disclosure and accounting treatment. Our procedures included inquiry of management, review of board minutes, and confirmation of transactions with related parties. All related party transactions appear to be properly disclosed in the financial statements.

Going Concern Evaluation
We evaluated the company's ability to continue as a going concern for a reasonable period of time. Our assessment included analysis of financial projections, debt covenant compliance, liquidity position, and management's plans to address any potential issues. We concluded that no substantial doubt exists regarding the company's ability to continue as a going concern.

Subsequent Events Review
We evaluated events occurring subsequent to the balance sheet date through our audit report date. Our procedures included inquiry of management, review of board minutes, and analysis of transactions or events that could materially affect the financial statements.

Management Letter Comments
During our audit, we identified certain matters involving internal control and other operational matters that we believe warrant management's attention. These observations and recommendations are communicated separately in our management letter dated March 15, 2025.

Audit Opinion
In our opinion, the consolidated financial statements referred to above present fairly, in all material respects, the financial position of XYZ Corporation and its subsidiaries as of December 31, 2024 and 2023, and the results of their operations and their cash flows for the years then ended in accordance with accounting principles generally accepted in the United States of America.

Critical Audit Matters
Critical audit matters are matters arising from the current period audit that were communicated to the audit committee and relate to accounts or disclosures that are material to the financial statements and involved especially challenging, subjective, or complex auditor judgment.

Significant Accounting Estimates
We evaluated the reasonableness of significant accounting estimates made by management including allowance for doubtful accounts, warranty reserves, asset retirement obligations, and pension liabilities. Our procedures included testing of underlying data, evaluation of assumptions, and independent calculation of estimates.

Compliance with Laws and Regulations
We evaluated the company's compliance with laws and regulations that could have a material effect on the financial statements. Our procedures included inquiry of management and legal counsel, review of correspondence with regulatory authorities, and assessment of the company's compliance monitoring procedures.

Quality Control and Engagement Team
This audit was conducted under our firm's quality control standards which require that we comply with ethical requirements and plan and perform the audit to obtain reasonable assurance about whether the financial statements are free of material misstatement. Our engagement team included partners and staff with appropriate experience and training in the client's industry.

Independence Declaration
We are required to be independent with respect to the company in accordance with the ethical requirements that are relevant to our audit of the financial statements, and we have fulfilled our other ethical responsibilities in accordance with these requirements.
